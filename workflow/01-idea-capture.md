# Stage 1 — Idea Capture

## Purpose

Record the raw idea, motivation, and initial vision before any analysis or refinement. The goal is to capture enough context that anyone (human or agent) can understand what you want to build and why.

---

## Required Inputs

- A human with an idea
- Willingness to write down the rough concept (even if incomplete)

---

## Key Questions to Answer

1. **What is the idea?** — Describe what you want to build in plain language
2. **Why does this matter?** — What problem does it solve? What value does it create?
3. **Who is it for?** — Who will use this? (Even if it's just you)
4. **What does success look like?** — How will you know it works?
5. **What exists already?** — Is there anything in place that this builds on or replaces?
6. **What constraints are known?** — Time, technology, integration requirements, etc.
7. **What is explicitly out of scope?** — What should this NOT do?

---

## Output / Artifact

**File**: `01-idea.md`
**Template**: [`templates/idea-intake.md`](../templates/idea-intake.md)

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] `01-idea.md` exists and is filled in with enough detail that someone unfamiliar could understand the idea
- [ ] The motivation and success criteria are stated (even if rough)
- [ ] Known constraints and scope boundaries are documented
- [ ] STATUS.md is updated to reflect completion of Stage 1

---

## Next Step

**→ [Stage 2: Clarification](02-clarification.md)**

The idea capture is reviewed for gaps, ambiguity, and missing information. The clarification stage uses structured Q&A to fill those gaps before writing a specification.

---

## Tips

- **Don't overthink it.** The idea doesn't need to be perfect. Capture what you have now.
- **Write for a stranger.** Imagine someone reading this with no prior context.
- **Note uncertainties.** If you're unsure about something, say so — that's valuable information for the clarification stage.
- **Avoid solution design.** This stage captures *what* and *why*, not *how*.
